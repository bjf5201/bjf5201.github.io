Welcome to my online portfolio! So glad you've stopped by. Here is where I keep a collection of everything I've been working on, from school work to passion projects. Check back for regular updates and new code releases.

A little bit about me. I am a Junior studying at UNC Wilmington in the Information Technology program. I began making websites as a teenager and it has always been a hobby of mine. After starting out as a marketing major, I realized it was silly to be spending hours on a "hobby" when I could be making a living off of it!

Born and raised in central North Carolina, I have loved tinkering with computers for as long as I can remember. I created my first website at the age of eleven, which was a fansite dedicated to Hilary Duff called "Hilary Heaven.com". I taught myself front end development languages through a process of trial, error, and a lot of Googling. Stack Overflow and Reddit became my best friends. After about a year I had taught myself HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

I took some time off from web development to pursue another passion of mine, acting, for seven years. While I was away from the world of the web, I still found myself creating graphics and websites every now and then to promote various shows I was a part of and I worked on some websites for my fellow acting friends and myself.

About a year ago I dusted off my keyboard and threw myself back into web development. What was once something I played around with for fun has now become somewhat of an obsession. I've polished my HTML, CSS, and JavaScript skills and have become familiar with Python and C#. The Bash command line is my preferred method of interacting with my machine and I'm learning how to write my own scripts for the shell.

This past winter, I had the pleasure of interning with ProCTS, a local professional technical consulting firm and while there learned about network and system administration.

I am eager to learn even more. What excites me about IT is that you simply never stop learning. THe knoweledge base grows exponentially with each day. I believe I would bring the proper technical skills and determination required for any entry-level IT role in an organization.
